<p><p>Soft tissue allografts are used in various medical procedures to repair and replace damaged or diseased soft tissues. There are three main types of soft tissue allografts: hyaline, elastic, and fibro. </p><p>- Hyaline grafts are composed of collagen fibers and provide a smooth and gliding surface in joints, typically used in cartilage repair.</p><p>- Elastic grafts contain elastin fibers that offer flexibility and stretchiness, frequently used in ligament and tendon repairs.</p><p>- Fibro grafts consist of fibrous tissue and are commonly employed for reconstructive surgeries, such as in repairing the anterior cruciate ligament in the knee.</p></p>
