<p><p>The induction furnace market consists of two main types: coreless induction furnaces and channel induction furnaces. Coreless induction furnaces use a coil positioned around a crucible to create an electromagnetic field that melts metal. These furnaces offer flexibility in terms of metal and alloy composition and are commonly used in foundries. On the other hand, channel induction furnaces use a channel-shaped crucible, which is surrounded by a coil. They are suitable for melting larger quantities of metal and are widely used in industries such as steelmaking and metal casting.</p></p>
